Service & Legacy

Edwin Andrews was the first Filipino post-war commander of the Philippine Air Force. He lost his life on May 18, 1947, when the C-47 transport plane 'Lili Marlene' crashed into Mt. Makaturing, Lanao Province.

Andrews is remembered for his leadership during the early post-war years and for shaping the future of the Philippine Air Force.

Honors

  • Edwin Andrews Air Base named in his honor
  • First Filipino post-war PAF Commander
  • Remembered for his commitment to the Philippine military
