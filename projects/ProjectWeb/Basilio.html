Biography & Service

Lieutenant Colonel Basilio Fernando was a pioneering Filipino airman and officer in the Philippine Army Air Corps. Before his aviation career, he served in the Philippine Constabulary and earned his commission as a 3rd Lieutenant in 1917.

Fernando died in a training accident on January 21, 1946, while flying a B-25 Mitchell bomber in Oklahoma, USA.

Achievements

  • Pioneering commander in Philippine military aviation
  • B-25 Mitchell training leader
  • Fernando Air Base in Lipa City named for him in 1948
